About Bukluran sa Kabuhayan

Bukluran sa Kabuhayan (BSK) is a Christ-centered livelihood organization that empowers communities through meaningful work and solidarity. Founded in November 2007 with support from the National Housing Authority and YMCA, BSK provides sustainable livelihood opportunities — especially for women — by transforming local materials into handcrafted goods.

The cooperative embodies hope and transformation. Through handmade bags, mats, and crafts, members of the community demonstrate that through faith and unity, economic abundance can grow even in humble beginnings.

Member of CCAP • Makers of handcrafted bags, baskets, and mats from abaca, jute, and raffia.

Our Backstory & Values

Bukluran sa Kabuhayan (BSK) is one of the newer organizations under the Community-based Cooperative and Advocacy Program (CCAP). It was formed in November 2007 through the assistance of the National Housing Authority and the Young Men’s Christian Association.

The goal is simple but powerful: provide livelihood opportunities to people in the community — especially women who were unemployed — by teaching and organizing them to produce marketable, handmade items. Today, the group turns local materials like abaca fiber, yarn, and raffia into baskets, bags, placemats, and doormats.

BSK wants to model to other communities that it is possible to move from economic deprivation to economic abundance when people work together, stay Christ-centered, and build a strong local economic base.

What we stand for

  • Christ-centered community. Our work is grounded in faith and service.
  • Livelihood for women. We create dignified income opportunities for the unemployed.
  • Economic transformation. From poverty to productivity; from dependence to abundance.
  • Local materials. Abaca, jute, and fiber turned into useful, beautiful products.
  • Community as model. We show that transformation is possible.
