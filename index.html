About Bukluran sa Kabuhayan

Bukluran sa Kabuhayan (BSK) is a Christ-centered livelihood organization that empowers communities through meaningful work and solidarity. Founded in November 2007, BSK provides sustainable livelihood opportunities — especially for women — by transforming local materials into handcrafted goods.

Through handmade bags, mats, and crafts, the community demonstrates that faith and unity can turn humble beginnings into stories of hope and transformation.

Our Backstory & Values

Bukluran sa Kabuhayan (BSK) was formed in November 2007 through the assistance of the National Housing Authority and the Young Men’s Christian Association. The goal is simple but powerful: to provide livelihood opportunities — especially for women — by teaching and organizing them to produce marketable, handmade items.

Today, the group turns local materials like abaca fiber, yarn, and raffia into baskets, bags, placemats, and doormats — showing that transformation is possible through collaboration and faith.

What we stand for

  • Christ-centered community. Our work is grounded in faith and service.
  • Livelihood for women. We create dignified income opportunities for the unemployed.
  • Economic transformation. From poverty to productivity; from dependence to abundance.
  • Local materials. Abaca, jute, and fiber turned into useful, beautiful products.
  • Community as model. We show that transformation is possible.
